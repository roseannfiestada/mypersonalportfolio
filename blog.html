My Journey from Administrative Pro to Learning Web Developement and Software Testing

Hey there,

I wanted to share my journey of switching gears in my career. It's not the kind of story you'd usually hear in a corporate setting, but it's my story, and I hope it inspires you to chase your dreams too.

For years, I was the go-to person for all things administrative. You needed a spreadsheet sorted and a stack of papers organized? I was your guy. It was a stable gig, and I was good at it. But, like many people, I reached a point where I craved something more. Something different. Something challenging.

I tried to explore web development and software testing. Yep, I decided to dive headfirst into the tech world. Now, I know what you're thinking: "But you didn't have any experience!" And you're right, I didn't. But what I did have was determination, a passion for problem-solving, and a willingness to learn.

So, I started learning. I hit the websites and online courses, and I embraced the world of coding. HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python - they became my new friends. And let me tell you, it was tough. There were moments when I wanted to throw my laptop out the window. But I didn't. I persevered.

I built my first website, this website. It wasn't fancy, but it worked. That feeling of accomplishment, of creating something from scratch, was addictive. I wanted more.

As for software testing, I realized it was like being a detective in the digital world. I hunted for bugs, glitches, and inconsistencies. It required attention to detail, patience, and a curious mind - qualities I honed during my days in administrative tasks.

I didn't just learn the technical stuff, though. I also discovered the power of networking. I joined online tech communities, and connected with people who were light-years ahead of me in the tech game. And you know what? They were incredibly supportive. They shared resources, offered advice, and even gave me a virtual pat on the back when I needed it most.

Sure, it was a bit scary to leave the comfort of my administrative role, but it was the best decision I ever made. I found a new career that I'm passionate about, and it turns out my administrative skills weren't wasted - they're still handy for keeping things organized in the tech world.

So, here I am, a career shifter, proof that it's never too late to chase your dreams. If you're thinking about making a change, my advice is simple: Go for it. Embrace the challenge, learn as much as you can, and don't be afraid to ask for help.

Your journey may not be conventional, but it'll be uniquely yours. And who knows, you might just surprise yourself with what you can achieve.

Cheers to chasing dreams and never looking back!

[Rose Ann Fiestada]
